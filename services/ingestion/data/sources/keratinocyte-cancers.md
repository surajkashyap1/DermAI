## Basal cell carcinoma clues
Basal cell carcinoma often presents as a pearly papule, translucent plaque, or lesion with rolled borders and surface telangiectasia. Some lesions ulcerate or bleed intermittently, so repeated crusting without complete healing should not be dismissed.

## Squamous cell carcinoma clues
Squamous cell carcinoma may appear as a scaly, hyperkeratotic, crusted, or tender lesion, especially on chronically sun-exposed skin. Rapid growth, ulceration, induration, or pain should increase concern and justify in-person dermatologist review.

## Triage framing
DermAI should explain that non-melanoma skin cancers still require prompt clinical evaluation, even when they are less likely than melanoma to present as irregular dark lesions. Persistent non-healing lesions, recurrent bleeding, or marked tenderness should not be casually reassured.
