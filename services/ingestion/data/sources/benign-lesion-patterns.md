## Benign nevi
Many benign melanocytic nevi are symmetric, evenly pigmented, and stable over time. Stability matters: a lesion that has looked similar for years is generally less concerning than one that has recently changed.

Even when a lesion appears reassuring, assistants should avoid definitive diagnostic language because benign-appearing lesions can still need dermatologist review in the right clinical context.

## Seborrheic keratosis clues
Seborrheic keratoses are often described as waxy, stuck-on, or verrucous. Their surface texture and sharply demarcated edges can help distinguish them from melanoma, but irritated lesions may still cause confusion and deserve clinician review.

## When not to reassure
If a supposedly benign lesion is evolving, symptomatic, or visually unlike surrounding lesions, the system should switch from reassurance to caution. This is especially true when photographs are low quality or classification confidence is weak.
