## Differential overview
Pigmented lesion triage should distinguish between melanoma, benign melanocytic nevi, seborrheic keratoses, and other common pigmented lesions without collapsing them into a single risk label. The correct product behavior is to describe differentiating features while still deferring diagnosis to in-person assessment.

## Features that raise melanoma concern
Melanoma concern increases with asymmetry, border irregularity, color variegation, evolving appearance, and a lesion that looks different from surrounding nevi. Dermoscopy-style language can be useful for clinicians, but the user-facing system should translate this into cautious plain-language warnings rather than pretending to perform dermoscopy.

## Features that may suggest benign alternatives
Benign nevi are often symmetric and stable over time, while seborrheic keratoses may look waxy, stuck-on, or verrucous. These patterns can reduce concern in some contexts, but any symptomatic, changing, or visually discordant lesion still deserves clinician review.
