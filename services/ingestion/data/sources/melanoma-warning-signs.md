## ABCDE features
The ABCDE framework is commonly used to flag lesions that deserve prompt dermatologist review. Clinicians look for asymmetry, irregular borders, multiple colors, diameter growth, and recent evolution in shape, size, color, or symptoms.

Evolution is especially important because a lesion that is changing over weeks to months may be more concerning than a stable lesion with otherwise mild atypia. Symptoms such as bleeding, itching, ulceration, or persistent crusting should increase caution rather than reassure.

## Risk context
Melanoma concern is higher when a patient reports a personal history of melanoma, strong family history, intense ultraviolet exposure, or a lesion that looks different from the patient's other moles. This "ugly duckling" pattern is a practical signal in triage conversations.

Any lesion associated with rapid growth, spontaneous bleeding, pain, or systemic symptoms should trigger escalation instead of casual reassurance. A cautious assistant should encourage urgent in-person assessment when these patterns are described.
