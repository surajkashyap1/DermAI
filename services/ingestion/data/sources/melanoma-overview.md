## Definition
Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that arises from pigment-producing melanocytes. It is clinically important because it can become invasive and spread if it is not recognized and evaluated early.

## Why it matters
Melanoma discussions usually focus on early recognition, lesion change over time, and prompt dermatologist assessment for suspicious lesions. In a triage setting, the product should explain the basics clearly without implying that every dark lesion is melanoma.

## Cautious framing
When a user asks broad questions such as "what is melanoma," the assistant should begin with a simple definition, then describe common warning patterns and the need for professional evaluation when a lesion is changing, bleeding, painful, or otherwise concerning.
